3. Overhaul Cargo Purchasing
New ordering model
Replace crate-based ordering with item-by-item selection.
Cargo staff create a list of individual items.
System automatically:
Determines crate count
Bundles items into crates
Calculates cost and shipping time
I have this idea in my head that basically goes like:
Crates can have 10 items max.
Each item has a crate type that it wants, or can specify one at least. Freezers for meat, etc.
The crates you are sent each have different values in their return.
If you select items, it will start telling you what crates will be included. If you for example have 9 screwdrivers you will have a single crate. Add one banana, and a fridge crate will be added solely for that. If you then add 9 apples, they get put in the same crate.
Prices will dynamically become cheaper/pricier the more you order in a batch, to a limit. So a single screwdriver will cost say, 10 credits or something:
Batch purchase gets a 200 credit fine/surcharge/whatever that shrinks if you buy more things, up to maybe 20 items in a batch. Then it will be a direct price to price thing. Any more items will get start reducing the total price up to a 20% bulk discount
Additionally, a score will be calculated based on how efficiently the crates are filled out. If you have a low average crate fill, meaning like 5 different types of crates with only a single item each, the score will be lower, and the price will go up.
Benefits
More granular control
Less clutter
Easier balancing of costs and supply chains
Implementation notes
